How Can You Invest
Your Savings?

  1. Home
  2. Saving for Retirement(active memeber)

Investing your savings can help them grow.

The investment route you choose will depend on how involved you want to be and how much risk you’re willing to take. High risk funds offer a greater chance of growing your savings over the long term, but carry the risk of suddenly falling in value. Low risk funds are generally more stable, but don’t offer much chance for growth.

Which investment route will you take?

You can choose Lifestyle or Freestyle. If you don’t make a choice, your savings will be invested in the Plan’s default strategy – Lifestyle Diversified Growth. This default strategy is a medium to high risk strategy, and from 20 years to retirement you will start being transferred into less risky assets to protect its value.

Lifestyle manages your investments for you. It aims to grow your savings over time and moves to less risky investments as you approach your target retirement age.

Freestyle needs you to be more involved. You choose which funds to invest in from the Plan’s range and you are responsible for managing them over time.

Your target retirement age

If you invest your savings in a lifestyle strategy you should also choose your target retirement age. Your investments will start to be moved into lower-risk funds based on this age. If you don't make a choice, your retirement age will be the Plans normal retirement age (65 or your State Pension Age if you were auto-enrolled).

Lifestyle

Lifestyle Cautious Growth

This Lifestyle strategy invests in the Cautious Growth Fund in the early years. The underlying investments are mostly bonds with a smaller amount of other assets including shares, property, emerging market currency, cash and infrastracture. It aims to provide moderate growth over the long term whilst spreading the risk across a number of asset classes

Investing in this Lifestyle strategy may be suitable for you if you do not like talking a lot of investments risk and prefer a low-risk investment journey. However, there is a risk that your investments won't keep pace with inflation over the long term.

Charges you pay

Legal and General Investment Management (LGIM) has been apointed by the Trustees to manage the investments of your Pension account. LGIM charge for this service; the charges are accrued on a daily basis throughout each quarter and reflected within the unit price for each fund.

For example, the charge for the Cautious Growth Fund is 0.125% of fund value per year, which means that for each £1,000 invested in the Cautios Growth Fund the investment management charge is £1.25 per annum and this is recovered through an adjustment to the unit price.
